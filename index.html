The Backtracking Algorithm is a complicated brute-force method that solves guess and check problems like Sudoku. Starting from the first empty cell found, it recursively traverses the rest of the empty cells in row major order, substituting values starting from 1 to 9 and checking if its valid. The Statistics

Difficulty ranking:

Ranking of difficulty is caclulated with two points of data. One is the amount of cells or squares that are filled up. An input of less filled cells will be harder to solve than an input with more cells because of the nature of sudoku puzzles and how different cells affect the solution. The second data point is the amount of filled-in cells in each 3x3 grid. If there are more grids with less filled-in cells then it is harder to solve. For instance, an input where each grid only has two numbers filled-in will be harder to solve than an input with four numbers filled in per grid. (If you want to look at exactly how its ranked, the code will be in the "calculateDifficulty" function in my github repository )

However, due to the possibility of many solutions to a puzzle, especially with inputs with small amounts of filled-in cells the ranking can be inaccurate. For instance, an input with only 1 cell filled in can be hard or easy, depending on the solution you want.
